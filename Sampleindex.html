Know Your Breast, Know Your Health

Breast Self-Examination (BSE) Awareness Guide for Young Women

What is Breast Self-Examination (BSE)?

BSE is a simple way of looking at and feeling your breasts regularly to check for any unusual changes. When you know how your breasts normally look and feel, it becomes easier to notice any changes early.

BSE is only a self-awareness practice.
BSE is about knowing your body better — not diagnosing a disease.

Most breast changes are not cancer, especially in teenagers, but knowing your normal helps you notice when something is different.

Knowing What’s Normal for You Makes It Easier to Notice:

  • Changes in size or shape
  • Lumps or thickening
  • Skin dimpling or redness
  • Nipple changes or discharge

Why Is BSE Important? (Even During Adolescence)

  • Builds body awareness
  • Promotes responsible health habits
  • Encourages early help-seeking behavior
  • Reduces fear through knowledge
  • Empowers young women to take care of their health

Who Can Perform BSE?

According to health organizations:

  • Girls and women who have started menstruating
  • Young women (including teenagers) to build awareness
  • Adult women of all ages

Breast Self-Examination: How to Perform the Examination

Normal Breast Changes

Illustration showing the steps for proper breast self-examination.

1. Look (In Front of a Mirror)

  • Remove clothing above the waist
  • Stand in front of a mirror with arms at your sides
  • Observe both breasts for:
    • Changes in size or shape
    • Skin dimpling or redness
    • Swelling
    • Nipple inversion or discharge
  • Repeat inspection with:
    • Hands on hips
    • Arms raised above the head
    • Slight forward bending

2. Feel (While Lying Down)

  • Lie on your back
  • Place a pillow under your shoulder
  • Put one arm behind your head
  • Use the flat part of your three middle fingers of the opposite hand
  • Move fingers in circular motions using light, medium, and deep pressure
  • Follow an up-and-down (vertical strip) pattern from:
    • Armpit
    • Across the breast
    • Toward the middle of the chest
  • Cover the entire breast area
  • Repeat the same procedure on the other breast

3. Check the Armpits

  • Examine both armpits for lumps or thickening
  • Gently squeeze the nipple to check for unusual discharge

When Should You Do BSE?

  • Once every month

Best Time:

  • 3–5 days after the onset of menstruation
  • or about 7–10 days after your menstrual period starts

(Breasts are less tender or swollen during this time)

If You Don’t Have Regular Periods:

  • Choose the same day each month (e.g., 1st day of the month)

Consistency helps you notice changes more easily.

What to Look or Feel for During BSE

  • A new lump, mass, or thickening in or near the breast or in the underarm area
  • An unexplained change in the size or shape of the breast
  • Swelling or shrinkage of all or part of the breast (especially if on one side only)
  • Dimpling anywhere on the breast
  • Puckering in the skin of the breast
  • A nipple turned inward (inverted)
  • Breast or nipple pain

What To Do If You Notice Changes

  • Stay calm
  • Inform a parent or guardian
  • Consult a school nurse or doctor

Most breast lumps in teenagers are NOT cancer.

Healthy Habits for Breast Health

  • Balanced diet
  • Regular physical activity
  • Avoid smoking
  • Maintain healthy weight
